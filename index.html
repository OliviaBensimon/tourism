United Nations Headquarters

Designed by a team of international architects, led by Le Corbusier, Oscar Niemeyer, and Wallace Harrison. The site used to be slaughterhouses, the 17 acre plot of land was "donated" by John D. Rockefeller in 1946. Opened in 1950.

The city's evolving skyline

There’s been a surge in luxury high rises over the past couple of years. All around Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens, buildings are popping up all around – and the waterfront is prime real estate, with the best views of the city’s famed skyscrapers. As my grandmother says, "I’ll blink and they’ll build another."

Old buildings

My grandmother has this picture from 1931 hanging by the window in her den – it’s the view from a similar vantage point of their apartment. I recognize the buildings in front, the art deco skyscraper off to the corner, the curve of the southern tip of Manhattan island. The piers are gone, replaced by the FDR drive; skyscrapers taking the place of tenement buildings and slaughterhouses.
